Crispy Peanut Tofu

A tasty meal that is fairly healthy to boot.

Ingredients

  • 1 package extra firm tofu
  • 1 large broccoli crown
  • Vegetable oil
  • 1 cup white rice
  • 2 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1/2 cup peanut butter
  • 1 clove garlic (minced)
  • 1/2" piece of giner (minced)
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • Red pepper flakes
  • salt/pepper

Steps

  1. Remove the todu from the package and set between 2 cutting boards to squeeze out extra moisture
  2. While tofu is pressing, add rice into rice cooker with 1 cup water and start the rice cooker
  3. Preheat oven to 425 degrees F
  4. Prepare broccoli by chopping off florets off at the stalk. May need to cut larger florets into 2
  5. Move broccoli to a large bowl and toss with 1 tbsp oil and pepper and salt
  6. Place broccoli on baking sheet
  7. Remove tofu from press and pat dry with a paper towel
  8. Cut tofu into roughly 3/4 inch cubes (should be about 24 pieces)
  9. Move tofu into a bowl and lightly toss with the corn starch
  10. Heat oil in a large pan or skillet. You want the depth of the oil to be about half the heigh of the tofu cubes
  11. Place broccoli into oven and cook for about 15 minutes or until it has some nice browning
  12. Gently place the tofu in the hot oil and fry for about 5 minutes flip them and cook for another 5. They should be a nice golden brown
  13. Remove tofu from oil and set aside on a plate with a paper towl to absorb extra oil
  14. In a measuring cup or small bowl stir together the peanut butter, soysauce, rice vinegar, honey, garlic, ginger, red pepper flakes, and a 1/4 cup of water
  15. Place sauce in a pan over medium heat and cook to meld the flavors together. Add more water as needed for desired consistency
  16. Remove broccoli from the oven
  17. Plate up, first adding rice to the bowl, then tofu and broccoli. Shower everything in peanut sauce and enjoy!
